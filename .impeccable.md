## Design Context

### Users
5th-grade students (10–11 years old) and their parents/guardians at Escola Conde de Oeiras. Used primarily **at home** for math practice — on whatever device is available: phones, tablets, and laptops. Must work well at all screen sizes, with special attention to mobile. Parents receive results by email, so the summary screen matters to them too.

### Brand Personality
**Exciting, fair, rewarding.** Students should feel like they're on stage — every correct answer is a win, every session feels earned. The game-show metaphor (Who Wants to Be a Millionaire) is the core reference. The interface should feel like a professional, trustworthy production — not a classroom worksheet — while remaining age-appropriate and encouraging.

Three-word personality: **charged, honest, earned.**
- *Charged* — there's energy here, stakes, anticipation
- *Honest* — clear feedback, no tricks, respects the student's intelligence
- *Earned* — progress feels meaningful, rewards feel deserved

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark game-show with warm gold authority.** Deep navy background (`#060914`), gold CTAs and highlights (`#ffce6b`–`#ffe4a7`), cool blue/purple stage lighting. The spotlight glow metaphor (left blue, right purple, bottom gold) is central to the atmosphere and should be preserved. Glassmorphism cards are intentional — they create depth and layering that reinforces the "stage under lights" feeling.

**Anti-references:** Avoid anything that looks like:
- A generic school worksheet or LMS (grey cards, flat blue buttons, white backgrounds)
- A generic dashboard (hero metrics, sparklines, admin-panel aesthetics)
- Cheap mobile game (neon-on-black, cluttered, overwhelming)

**Theme:** Dark only. The night-time stage atmosphere is core to the identity.

### Design Principles
1. **Every right answer should feel like a moment** — feedback, transitions, and rewards should feel earned and satisfying, not clinical
2. **Respect the student's intelligence** — clear hierarchy, honest feedback, no patronising design choices
3. **Mobile is primary** — students use phones at home; nothing should break or feel cramped under 390px
4. **Depth over flatness** — layered backgrounds, glows, and translucency create the stage atmosphere; preserve this intentionally rather than flattening it
5. **Gold means important** — the gold/yellow palette (`#ffce6b`) should only appear on primary CTAs and key achievements; overuse dilutes its authority
